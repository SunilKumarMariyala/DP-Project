Fault Types Reference

The solar panel is operating within normal parameters. Current and voltage readings are within expected ranges based on environmental conditions.

Characteristics: Stable voltage output, current proportional to irradiance, normal power production.

A line-line fault occurs when there is an unintended connection between two points in the circuit, creating a short circuit.

Characteristics: Significant voltage drop, increased current flow, reduced power output.

Recommended Action: Inspect wiring connections, check for damaged insulation, and verify junction box integrity.

An open circuit fault occurs when there is a break in the electrical path, preventing current flow in part or all of the panel.

Characteristics: Abnormally high voltage, significantly reduced or zero current, minimal power output.

Recommended Action: Check for broken connections, inspect for cell cracks, and test for continuity across the panel.

Partial shading occurs when a portion of the panel is blocked from sunlight, creating hotspots and reducing efficiency.

Characteristics: Multiple power peaks, reduced current, uneven voltage distribution across cells.

Recommended Action: Remove physical obstructions, clean panel surface, and consider repositioning to avoid regular shading patterns.

Panel degradation is the gradual reduction in performance over time due to environmental factors, material breakdown, or manufacturing defects.

Characteristics: Consistently lower power output than rated, gradual decline in efficiency, normal voltage but reduced current.

Recommended Action: Perform detailed I-V curve analysis, check for discoloration or delamination, and consider replacement if efficiency drops below acceptable levels.
